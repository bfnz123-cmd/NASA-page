The Human Flight Log

Look up. Right now, hundreds of miles above your head, a new class of explorers is staring back.


Some are career scientists conducting life-saving research in continuous orbit. Others are civilian pioneers who took a chance on a livestreamed ticket,
feeling the crushing weight of several Gs before floating into three minutes of silent, weightless awe.
The barrier to the stars hasn’t just leaked; it has broken wide open. Whether it is SpaceX charting historic polar trajectories,
Blue Origin advancing universal accessibility, or NASA laying the foundation for permanent lunar colonies,
space is becoming a human domain—not just a national one.
Step inside the logistics, the economics, and the human stories of the men and women currently breaking free from Earth’s gravity.

  • Government/Institutional Programs: Multi-month orbital stays executing microgravity science and planetary transit research.

  • Private & Commercial Commercial Flights: Quick suborbital edges-of-space or private orbital flights funded by individuals or private corporations.
