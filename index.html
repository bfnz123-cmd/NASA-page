The Human Flight Log

Look up. Right now, hundreds of miles above your head, a new class of explorers is staring back.

Some are career scientists conducting life-saving research in continuous orbit. Others are civilian pioneers who took a chance on a livestreamed ticket, feeling the crushing weight of several Gs before floating into three minutes of silent, weightless awe.

The barrier to the stars has broken wide open. Whether it is SpaceX charting historic polar trajectories, Blue Origin advancing universal accessibility, or NASA laying the foundation for permanent lunar colonies, space is becoming a human domain—not just a national one.
